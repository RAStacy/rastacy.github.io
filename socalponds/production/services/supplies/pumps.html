Pumps

There are many different choices when selecting a pump. Do you choose an internal pump? What about an external pump? Will this pump be large enough for my desired waterflow? How do I know which sized pump I need? These are all questions you need to ask yourself when selecting a pump for you pond.

Lets start with external pumps, I prefer external pumps over internal or submersible pumps because it allows for different plumbing configurations, a collection point for debris and usually requires less electricity… a.k.a. lower electric bill. What is an external pump? An external pump is similar to a pump you would see installed on a traditional swimming pool, a pump that is installed outside of the pond. External pumps are ideal when there is limited space inside the pond, multiple suctions points or you just want to move a lot of water.

Internal pumps or submersible pumps are pumps that can be placed directly in the pond under the water. These pumps are usually located in the ponds skimmer. These pumps are ideal when you have a smaller simple pond where there is only one means of suction, usually the skimmer.

There are submersible pumps that range from 20 g.p.h. up to 10,000 g.p.h. and higher.
